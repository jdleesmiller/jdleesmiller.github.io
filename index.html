About

  • He is a co(de)founder at Overleaf, an online editor that helps scientists write papers collaboratively.
  • He was a post-doc at the University of Southampton, and then the University of Bristol, where he worked on ways of cheaply tracking cars through road networks to help us understand how people make travel decisions.
  • He did a PhD on how to efficiently operate fleets of driverless cars, so we'll be ready when they get here. That was in the engineering mathematics department at the University of Bristol.
  • He was part of the team that delivered the world's first Personal Rapid Transit system at London's Heathrow Airport in 2011 — computer-guided taxis on dedicated roads.
  • He studied computing science at the University of Alberta.
